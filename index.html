  • About Me

    Who am I?

    I'm Yu-Kuan (pronounced "you koo-a-n", not "you ka-n" like the Yukon Potato), or Cliff, currently a graduate student at the School of Information at the University of Texas at Austin, aiming to be a UX designer/researcher. With my bachelor's in psychology, I hope to contribute to the field by combining my knowledge in human behavior, perception, and cognition with the UX skillsets I'm learning right now.

    Since I'm a huge basketball fan, I was trilled when I went to see my first NBA game, the season opener of the San Antonio Spurs against the Minnesota Timberwolves. Even more memorable was that I had the chance to shoot a free throw on the court. To this day, my free throw percentage on the NBA court is 100%.

    My interest in UX began when I took Design Psychology in my senior year. The class emphasized on how designs can influence the human mind and thus increase work efficiency and reduce human error, and I became intrigued in the concept.

    Logo Design Concept

    My full name is Yu-Kuan Hwang, so I took each of the captialized letter into account when I created my logo. Initially, the Y and K will stand out, the H of my last name, on the contrary, is placed in the middle. It won't obvious at first, since it uses the Gestalt's principle of closure, meaning people will see complete figures through the context even though parts of the information might be missing.
